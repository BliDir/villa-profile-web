About This Space

Bathing Under Open Sky

The outdoor bathroom is the villa's most unconventional space — and, for many guests, its most memorable. A deep stone soaking tub sits at the centre of a semi-open room, shielded from view by a living wall of bamboo and banana palms that filter the light and absorb the sounds of the outside world.

A generous rain shower occupies one corner, fed by a wide overhead head that turns rinsing off into something worth staying under. The stone basin is paired with a timber shelf stocked with organic coconut-based toiletries — shampoo, conditioner, body wash, and a bar soap made locally. Nothing is in plastic.

The space is designed to slow you down. Thick cotton towels and a folded bathrobe wait on the stone bench. A candle holder sits beside the tub. There is no speaker, no phone shelf, no rush. Just open sky, warm water, and the sound of leaves.
