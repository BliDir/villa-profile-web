About This Space

A Proper Kitchen

Not all villa kitchens are created equal. This one was designed for guests who actually cook. A six-burner gas range sits beneath a stainless steel hood, flanked by generous prep surfaces in pale stone. Pots, pans, and knives are the proper kind — not the too-light, too-light versions that disappoint you the moment you pick them up.

The central island doubles as a breakfast bar, with three bar stools that fill up fast during morning coffee. An espresso machine, filtered water tap, and a dedicated juice bar make slow mornings effortless. The large-capacity fridge is stocked with essentials on arrival and has room for everything you might bring back from the local market.

A garden-facing window runs the length of the prep sink — the best view in the villa, some guests say — and everything from the dishwasher to the rice cooker is thoughtfully placed within reach. Whether you're cooking for two or feeding the whole villa, this kitchen handles it.
